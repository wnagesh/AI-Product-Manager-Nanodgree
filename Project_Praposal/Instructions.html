Image Categorization

Instructions

Overview

Help doctors quickly determine if there are pneumonia symptoms in the images we provide.

Steps

  1. Examine the image.
  2. If there are pneumonia symptoms in the image, check the "yes" radio button.
  3. If there aren't any pneumonia symptoms., check the "no" radio button.
  4. If you are not sure of whether there are pneumonia symptoms or not, check the "unknown" radio button.



Rules Tips

Rules:

  • Only look for pneumonia symptoms.
  • pneumonia symptoms
    • A pneumonia image may include a few things: areas of cloudiness/opacity in several concentrated areas or one large area. You may also see a general pattern of opacity that obscures the structure of the lungs, heart, and diaphragm.
  • healthy symptoms
    • A normal, healthy image will depict clear lungs without any areas of abnormal cloudiness/opacity; there may be structured, web-like vasculature in the lungs but otherwise that area should be clear. In healthy images, you are also more likely to see a diaphragm shadow.
  • If you are uncertain, dont take a chance, please choose the unknown button.
  • In any case, do not select two buttons/options.
